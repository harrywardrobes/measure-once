MEASURE ONCE — CUSTOMER PIPELINE REFERENCE
==========================================
Purpose: Documents the complete customer pipeline from new lead through to
READY_FOR_PRODUCTION. Intended audience: human team members and AI agents
assisting with pipeline tasks.

This document covers three pipeline stages — Sales, Design Visit, and Survey —
and stops at READY_FOR_PRODUCTION. Stages beyond that point (Order, Workshop,
Packing, Delivery, Installation, Aftercare) are outside the scope of this
document.


SYSTEMS INVOLVED
----------------

Measure Once Dashboard
  The internal web application where staff manage the customer pipeline.
  Displays a Kanban-style board driven by HubSpot lead status. Staff use it to
  send the customer-info form link, view photo submissions, manage design visit
  records, schedule visits, and advance customers through stages.

HubSpot CRM
  The authoritative record of each customer (contact). One custom property
  drives the pipeline display in the dashboard:
    hs_lead_status      — primary pipeline status (e.g. IN_PROGRESS, SCHEDULED)
  The dashboard reads this property to place each contact on the board.
  Staff update them via the dashboard or directly in HubSpot. All CRM notes
  are written back to HubSpot automatically on certain events (e.g. design
  visit submission).

QuickBooks
  Used for financial tracking. Estimates are created automatically by the
  dashboard when a design visit is submitted. Invoices (including deposit
  invoices) are created manually by staff inside QuickBooks; the dashboard
  reads and displays outstanding invoice balances but does not create invoices
  itself.

Customer-Info Form
  A token-gated public web page sent to the customer during the Sales stage.
  Collects contact details, address, number of rooms, room notes, and photos.
  The link is personal to the customer and expires after 28 days. Staff trigger
  it via the dashboard. On submission it notifies admins by email and can
  automatically advance the customer's HubSpot lead status.

Design Visit Sign-Off Page
  A token-gated public web page sent to the customer after a design visit is
  submitted. Shows the room breakdown and pricing summary. The customer signs
  off their design by clicking an approval button on this page. The token
  expires after 7 days and is rotated each time the designer re-submits the
  visit. Old tokens are retained as superseded hashes so the system can show
  the customer a helpful "designer is making changes" message rather than a
  generic error if they click a stale link.

Google Calendar
  Used to schedule visits (design visits, survey visits, and other appointment
  types). Calendar events are created from within the dashboard and optionally
  synced to Google Calendar via OAuth.

Email (SMTP)
  Transactional emails sent by the dashboard: the customer-info form invite,
  the customer thank-you after form submission, the design visit sign-off link,
  and admin notification emails when a customer submits their info form.


STAGE 1 — SALES
---------------

This stage covers the period from first contact through to a confirmed design
visit appointment.

HubSpot lead statuses in pipeline order:

1. FORM_SUBMISSION
   The customer has submitted an initial enquiry (e.g. via the website contact
   form). Staff action: email and WhatsApp the customer to ask for more
   information. This is the entry point for all new leads.

2. ATTEMPTED_CONTACT
   Staff have tried to reach the customer but received no reply. Staff action:
   if no response within 2 working days, call to discuss. The customer-info
   form link is often sent at this stage to gather details asynchronously.

3. IN_PROGRESS
   Active conversation is underway. The lead is being qualified.

4. AWAITING_PHOTOS
   Staff are waiting for the customer to submit their photos and home details
   via the customer-info form.

   Customer-Info Form Flow:
   - Staff click "Send info form" on the dashboard contact card.
   - The dashboard generates a unique token, stores its hash with a 28-day
     expiry, and emails the customer a personal link.
   - The customer opens the link and fills in: corrected email, corrected
     mobile, address, number of rooms (1 / 2 / 3+), room notes, and photos
     (up to 15 images, JPEG/PNG/WebP).
   - On submission: the dashboard stores the answers, emails an admin
     notification (with photos attached where possible), emails the customer a
     thank-you, and updates hs_lead_status in HubSpot.
   - The admin notification includes all submitted details and any photos that
     fit within the email size limit. Large photos are skipped with a note to
     view them on the dashboard.

5. ROUGH_ESTIMATE
   Staff have reviewed the photos and sent a rough price estimate to the
   customer.

   Photo Review Flow (two outcomes):
   a) rough_estimate_sent — The rooms and photos look suitable. Staff send a
      rough estimate and advance the status to ROUGH_ESTIMATE.
   b) not_suitable — The enquiry is not a good fit (e.g. property type,
      scale, or scope). Staff mark the lead NOT_SUITABLE (see closed exits
      below). The customer may be informed by email outside the dashboard.

Closed (exit) statuses — these can be applied at any point in Sales:

   UNQUALIFIED
     The customer does not meet the basic criteria for a project (e.g. budget
     too low, property type out of scope). No further action is taken.

   NOT_SUITABLE
     The enquiry is unsuitable for a specific reason identified after more
     information was gathered (e.g. after photo review). Distinct from
     UNQUALIFIED in that a qualification step was reached before the decision.

   BAD_TIMING
     The customer is interested but not ready now. Staff note a follow-up date
     (typically 1 month or a date the customer suggested) and recontact later.
     The lead may be re-entered into the active pipeline at that point.


STAGE 2 — DESIGN VISIT
-----------------------

This stage covers the scheduling, delivery, and customer sign-off of a design
visit. A design visit is an in-home aesthetic consultation where the designer
discusses furniture styles, handles, door finishes, room layouts, and pricing.
It is distinct from the Survey visit (see Stage 3).

HubSpot lead statuses in pipeline order:

1. SCHEDULED
   A design visit appointment has been booked. The date is added to the shared
   calendar from the dashboard.

2. OPEN_DEAL
   The design visit has taken place and a design visit record has been submitted.
   The deal is open and the customer's sign-off is awaited (or has been received).

Design Visit Record Statuses (internal, stored in the design_visits table):

The design visit record is separate from the HubSpot contact status. It tracks
the state of the design document itself.

  draft
    The designer has started a design visit record but has not yet submitted it.
    Rooms, pricing, handles, furniture range, and door styles can be edited
    freely. No QB estimate exists yet.

  submitted
    The designer has submitted the record. This triggers all side effects:
      - Status field updated to "submitted" in the database.
      - A new sign-off token is generated and emailed to the customer (7-day
        link to the public sign-off page).
      - An old token (if any) is moved to superseded_signoff_token_hashes.
      - A HubSpot CRM note is created summarising the visit details and room
        breakdown, associated with the contact.
      - hs_lead_status in HubSpot may be updated (if the submitter is a
        manager or admin and submittedLeadStatus is configured).
      - A QuickBooks estimate is created (or updated — see QB estimate logic
        below).

  revision_requested
    After reviewing the sign-off page, the customer has requested changes. The
    designer edits the record (rooms, pricing, styles) and resubmits. This
    status can cycle multiple times. Each resubmission:
      - Generates a new sign-off token (old one is superseded).
      - Re-runs the full side-effect chain (HubSpot note, QB estimate update).
      - The revision_note field records the customer's change request.

  signed_off
    The customer has approved the design via the public sign-off page. The
    signed_off_at timestamp is recorded. The pipeline then advances toward
    the Survey stage (staff set hs_lead_status to DESIGN_ACCEPTED in HubSpot
    when they are ready to proceed).

QuickBooks Estimate Logic (triggered on submit / resubmit):

  Each room in the design visit maps to one line item in the QB estimate:
    - Description: room name, door style, dimensions, and unit count
    - Amount:      unit_price * unit_count (converted from pence to GBP)
    - ItemRef:     "Design & Fit" service item
    - TxnDate:     today's date
    - CustomerRef: the HubSpot contact ID (used as the QB customer reference)
    - CustomerMemo: "Design visit — [contact name]\nHandle: ...\nFurniture range: ..."
    - ExpirationDate: visit date + 30 days

  On first submission: a new estimate is created and its QB estimate ID and
  document number are stored on the design_visits row.

  On resubmission (revision_requested → resubmit):
    - If the prior estimate still has TxnStatus = "Pending" (or no status),
      the dashboard issues a sparse update to the existing estimate. The
      qb_estimate_id and qb_estimate_doc_num remain unchanged.
    - If the prior estimate is in any other state (Accepted, Closed, Rejected,
      voided, deleted, or otherwise unreachable), a new estimate is created.
      The old estimate ID is appended to the qb_estimate_history JSONB column
      with a replaced_at timestamp, replaced_by user, and reason field set to
      "prior_estimate_not_updatable". The qb_estimate_id is updated to the
      new estimate.

Stale Sign-Off Link (edge case):

  If the customer clicks an old sign-off link (from a previous submission or
  resubmission), the public sign-off route recognises the token as superseded
  (present in superseded_signoff_token_hashes) and returns a 409 response with
  a message explaining that the designer is making changes. This prevents the
  customer from approving an outdated design.

No-Show Visit (edge case):

  There is no formal system status for a customer who does not attend their
  design visit appointment. Staff handle no-shows operationally: a HubSpot
  note is added manually, the appointment is rescheduled via the calendar, and
  the HubSpot status may remain SCHEDULED or be adjusted to reflect the
  situation. The dashboard does not have an automatic no-show workflow.


STAGE 3 — SURVEY
-----------------

This stage covers the period from design sign-off through to the point where
the project is ready to enter production. The survey is a separate physical
visit from the design visit. Where the design visit is about aesthetics and
pricing, the survey is a technical measurement visit to capture exact
dimensions for manufacturing.

HubSpot lead statuses in pipeline order:

1. DESIGN_ACCEPTED
   The customer has signed off the design. The pipeline has formally moved from
   the Design Visit stage into the Survey stage.

2. AWAITING_DEPOSIT
   Staff are waiting for the customer to pay a deposit invoice. Deposit invoices
   are created manually by staff inside QuickBooks; the dashboard displays the
   outstanding balance fetched from QuickBooks but does not create the invoice
   itself. Staff advance the status manually in the dashboard once payment is
   confirmed.

3. SCHEDULED (Survey stage)
   The survey visit has been booked. The appointment is added to the calendar.

4. IN_PROGRESS
   The survey is underway. Staff action: check the date with the customer
   and confirm the surveyor is on site.

5. READY_FOR_PRODUCTION
   The survey has been completed and all technical measurements have been
   confirmed. Staff action: email the customer to confirm the installation date.
   This status is advanced manually by staff after verifying that the survey
   data is complete and correct.

   READY_FOR_PRODUCTION is the endpoint of this document. It signals that the
   job is ready to be handed to the Order and Workshop stages.


TRANSITION: READY_FOR_PRODUCTION TO WORKSHOP
--------------------------------------------

Once a customer is at READY_FOR_PRODUCTION, the project enters the production
pipeline. The stages beyond this point (outside the scope of this document) are:

  Order     — Staff order doors, sheet materials, and hardware.
  Workshop  — Staff prepare the installer pack, print labels, cut and prep all
              components, and notify the customer that production is underway.
  Packing   — Components are wrapped, a delivery date is agreed, and the van
              is loaded.
  Delivery  — Components are delivered to site.
  Installation — The installation team fits everything on site, completes a
              sign-off with the customer, and the final invoice is sent.
  Aftercare — Final payment is received and a thank-you message is sent.

KEY FIELD REFERENCE
-------------------

HubSpot Contact Properties:

  hs_lead_status
    Primary pipeline position. Values correspond to the statuses listed in the
    Pipeline Summary Table above. This is the field the dashboard board reads
    to place a contact in the correct column.

Design Visits Table Fields (design_visits):

  status
    Current lifecycle state of the design visit record. One of:
      draft, submitted, revision_requested, signed_off

  revision_note
    Text recorded when a customer requests changes. Populated when the sign-off
    page receives a revision request. May be updated on each revision cycle.

  signoff_token_hash
    SHA-256 hash of the current active sign-off token. The raw token is
    embedded in the link emailed to the customer. The server only stores the
    hash and never the raw token.

  signoff_expires_at
    Expiry timestamp of the current active sign-off token (7 days from
    submission). The public sign-off page rejects expired tokens.

  superseded_signoff_token_hashes
    Array of SHA-256 hashes for all previously issued (now invalidated) sign-off
    tokens on this visit. Used to distinguish a stale-but-valid-format link
    (show a helpful message) from an unrecognised token (generic not-found).

  qb_estimate_id
    QuickBooks estimate ID for the current estimate. Updated on each successful
    estimate create or sparse update.

  qb_estimate_doc_num
    QuickBooks document number (human-readable estimate reference, e.g. "1042").
    Displayed in the dashboard alongside the estimate ID.

  qb_estimate_history
    JSONB array of objects, one entry per replaced estimate. Each entry contains:
      qb_estimate_id      — the old estimate's QB ID
      qb_estimate_doc_num — the old estimate's document number
      replaced_at         — ISO timestamp when it was replaced
      replaced_by         — email of the submitter who triggered the replacement
      reason              — always "prior_estimate_not_updatable"


NOTES FOR AI AGENTS
-------------------

1. hs_lead_status is the primary pipeline signal. When determining where a
   customer is in the pipeline, always read hs_lead_status from HubSpot first.

2. Design visit and survey visit are two different physical appointments.
   A "design visit" (Stage 2) is an aesthetic consultation about styles,
   handles, finishes, and pricing — it produces a design visit record in the
   database and a QB estimate. A "survey visit" (Stage 3) is a separate
   technical measurement visit that happens after the customer has signed off
   the design and paid a deposit. Do not conflate the two.

3. QB estimates are created automatically on design visit submission; invoices
   are created manually by staff in QuickBooks. The dashboard can display
   outstanding invoice balances (read from QuickBooks) but it does not create
   or send invoices. Deposit invoices are issued manually by staff once a
   customer reaches AWAITING_DEPOSIT.

4. A signed-off design does not mean the customer is READY_FOR_PRODUCTION.
   After the customer approves the design (signed_off), the pipeline still
   requires: staff to advance to DESIGN_ACCEPTED, the customer to pay a
   deposit (AWAITING_DEPOSIT), a survey visit to be scheduled and conducted
   (SCHEDULED → IN_PROGRESS), and staff to manually confirm the survey data
   before advancing to READY_FOR_PRODUCTION.

5. revision_requested can cycle multiple times. A customer may request changes
   and the designer may resubmit more than once before the customer signs off.
   Each resubmission rotates the sign-off token and re-runs the full side-effect
   chain (HubSpot note, QB estimate update). The qb_estimate_history field
   records the audit trail of any replaced estimates across these cycles.

6. READY_FOR_PRODUCTION is a manual staff advancement. The system does not
   automatically advance a customer to READY_FOR_PRODUCTION after the survey
   visit is marked complete. Staff must confirm the survey data is correct
   and then manually move the customer to this status in the dashboard.
